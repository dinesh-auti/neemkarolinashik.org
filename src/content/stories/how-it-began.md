It began with no plan at all. A few devotees, scattered across Nashik, each quietly holding Maharaj-ji in their hearts, found one another. One offered his office space after work hours. Someone brought a photo, someone brought a diya, someone knew the Hanuman Chalisa by heart.

The first Tuesday there were a handful of us. The next, a few more. Word travels the way it always has among bhaktas — gently, person to person. Now the room is fuller each week.

We are not building an institution. We are simply keeping a Tuesday open, lighting a lamp, and singing — and trusting Maharaj-ji to bring whoever is meant to come.
